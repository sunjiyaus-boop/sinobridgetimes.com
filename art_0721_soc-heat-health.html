Watch for Blood-Pressure Spikes and Dizziness in Heat: 'Heat, Dehydration and Mood Swings All Play a Part'

Cases of sudden blood-pressure spikes and dizziness are rising in the heat. Doctors advise that heat, dehydration and emotional swings can all be triggers. We deliver health information without language barriers.
By Yu Seon-gyeong · 2026.07.21

Blood-pressure spikes and complaints of dizziness are increasing in high-heat weather, United Daily reported, with doctors explaining that dehydration and emotional agitation—not just heat itself—can be triggers.

광고 문의 · 300×250

In summer, sweating drains water and electrolytes, affecting blood pressure and circulation; those with hypertension or cardiovascular disease and the elderly need special care.

For prevention, ample hydration, avoiding midday outdoor activity, resting in cool places and taking medication regularly are advised; seek care at once if symptoms are severe.

Extreme heat burdens the mind as well as the body—adequate rest and calm are part of health care too.
