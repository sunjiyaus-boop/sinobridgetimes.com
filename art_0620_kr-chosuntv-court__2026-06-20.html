Court: 'Over-Reporting North Korean TV Is Not Grounds to Cancel Registration'—Speech and Press at Issue

A court ruled that a media outlet's registration cannot be canceled merely for over-reporting North Korean media content, raising the line between press freedom and regulation.
By Jo Gye-hyeon · 2026.06.20

A court ruled that registration cannot be canceled solely for over-reporting content from North Korean media such as Korean Central TV, Chosun Ilbo reported—a judgment on whether the volume and manner of coverage can ground administrative sanction.

광고 문의 · 300×250

The case asks where editorial discretion ends and administrative regulation begins. What and how much to report is, in principle, within editorial freedom; state sanction after the fact requires clear legal grounds and proportionality.

At the same time, citing and re-citing North Korean media carries separate legal and social considerations. The court is read as holding that the sheer volume of coverage can hardly justify so grave a sanction as canceling registration.

This paper relays the balance of free expression and responsibility on the facts. As an outlet handling many languages and sources, the standard for citation and reporting is an important theme for us, too.
