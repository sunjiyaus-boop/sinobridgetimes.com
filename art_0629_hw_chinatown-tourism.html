Incheon Chinatown, a 'living museum' of tourism and commerce

Incheon's Chinatown continues to thrive as a tourist destination and a Chinese commercial district. With history, food and culture intertwined, it is called a 'living museum.'
By Son Seung-jong · 2026.06.29

Chinatown before Incheon Station is a street formed with the settlement of Chinese during the port-opening era, a tourism and commercial district dense with restaurants and shops.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Known as the birthplace of jjajangmyeon, it is a space layered with the community's social history. On weekends, domestic and foreign tourists stream in.

Local government and merchants seek to revitalize the district by improving the streetscape and developing content. Balancing identity preservation with commercialization is a task.

Sinophone Bridge Times illuminates Chinatown as a living space of the Chinese community, beyond a tourist site.
