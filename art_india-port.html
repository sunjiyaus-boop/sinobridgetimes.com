A 48-Team World Cup: Eight Arab Nations Qualify Together; Curacao Sets 'Smallest' Mark

The expanded 2026 World Cup finals feature 48 nations for the first time. Eight Arab states qualified together, and Cape Verde, Curacao, Jordan and Uzbekistan reached the finals for the first time.
By Kang Pae-san · 2026.06.10

Expanding from 32 to 48 teams, the 2026 World Cup symbolizes diversity. From the Arab and Middle Eastern world, Algeria, Egypt, Iraq, Jordan, Morocco, Qatar, Saudi Arabia and Tunisia qualified together—double the four of the previous two editions.
