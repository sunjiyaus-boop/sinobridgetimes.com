Calls to Reform Korea's 'Decade-Old Football Cartel' Reignite

Criticism of a 'decade-old cartel' within the Korea Football Association and demands for reform have flared again, Chosun Ilbo reported Sunday, as repeated administrative controversies deepen fan distrust.
By Yeo Nyeon-jae · 2026.07.18

At the core is a decision-making structure built on personal networks, with transparency lacking in coach appointments, refereeing administration and youth-system investment.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Reform proposals include a standing coach-selection committee with outside members, a diversified board and expanded financial disclosure.

Each World Cup cycle, administrative disputes entangle with on-field results, amplifying the conflict.

Fan groups say the problem is the system, not results, pledging sustained scrutiny, while lawmakers discuss governance reform for sports bodies.

Transparency in sports administration is a shared concern in Taiwanese and Chinese football, making this a potential touchstone for East Asian governance reform.
