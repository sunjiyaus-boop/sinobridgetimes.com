Fisherman-Vendor Who Studied Nights for 10 Years Tops Taiwan Civil-Service Exam List

A Taiwanese examinee born in 1976, who fished by day, ran a street stall and studied by night for a decade, made the honor roll of the local civil-service special examination, the United Daily News reported. He credits 'two mindsets' for turning his life around.
By Jeon Geum-mun · 2026.07.17

His formula is simple: the constancy to finish a set amount daily, and the resilience to sit back down after failing.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Juggling livelihood and study, he failed several times but kept revising his methods and shoring up weak subjects.

Taiwan's civil-service exams are fiercely competitive with many long-term repeaters, and midlife success stories resonate widely.

In an aging era, late challenges are a trend, not an exception — raising the stakes for systems that support lifelong learning.

Age is a number and the desk is always there to return to; that is how many are reading his pass.
