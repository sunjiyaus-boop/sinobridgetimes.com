Russia Bans Diesel Exports After Ukraine's 'Industrial Scale' Strikes on Refineries, Tankers

Russia banned diesel exports after Ukraine hit 21 tankers and multiple refineries with drones over three days in what officials called an 'industrial scale' offensive, causing fuel shortages across Russia, Fox News and others reported.
By Yeo Nyeon-jae · 2026.07.14

Ukraine's long-range drone campaign has reportedly cut Russian gasoline output to about 65% of normal levels.

광고 문의 · 300×250

The ban is a stopgap to secure domestic fuel, but countries reliant on Russian diesel face inevitable supply shocks.

If global diesel prices swing, costs could pass through logistics and farming into consumer prices — another variable for the world economy.

Reuters carried analysis that strikes on energy assets may only harden President Putin's resolve to press the war.

The battlefield is now refinery towers and ports, and the energy-infrastructure war will shape the conflict's next phase.
