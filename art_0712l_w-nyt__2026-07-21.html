Federal Agents Serve NYT Reporters Grand Jury Subpoenas Over Air Force One Leak Probe

The Trump administration served New York Times journalists with grand jury subpoenas at their homes, seeking sources behind reporting on the Qatar-supplied Boeing 747-8 Air Force One — stirring a press-freedom fight.
By Ma Yak-ran · 2026.07.21

Federal agents delivered grand jury subpoenas to New York Times reporters at their homes, U.S. outlets including Fox News reported, in a probe of leaks behind reporting on the new 747-8 presidential aircraft from Qatar.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Directly subpoenaing reporters collides with source-protection norms and is rare even in the U.S. Press groups warn of a chilling effect on newsgathering.

The Times is said to be weighing legal options and stressing the public interest of its reporting; the administration calls it a legitimate national-security leak investigation.

Tensions between the administration and major outlets have simmered for months; the subpoenas move that conflict into the courts.

A legal battle over the line between First Amendment protections and leak inquiries now looks likely.
