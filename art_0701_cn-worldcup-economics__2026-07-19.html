The economics of the 'golden ticket'—the ledger behind the 2026 World Cup

An analysis around the 2026 World Cup examines the gap between the box-office hopes dubbed a 'golden ticket' and actual economic effects, arguing the costs and benefits of hosting and competing deserve a cool reckoning.
By Yu Jong-han · 2026.07.19

The World Cup sparks huge attention and spending, but whether it 'pays' once infrastructure investment and operating costs are counted varies by case, a Huanqiu analysis said.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Short term, tourism, broadcast and ad demand rise, but for big investments like stadiums and transport, post-event 'use' is the key; idle facilities leave only a burden.

For firms it is both a marketing chance and a cost; sponsorship pays but competition is fierce, making returns hard to gauge.

Experts say a mega event's success rests not on 'hosting itself' but on later asset use and city operations.
