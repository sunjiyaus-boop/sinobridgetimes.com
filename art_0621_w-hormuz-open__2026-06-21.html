다만 협상과 군사적 긴장이 동시에 진행되는 국면에서 해협의 안정은 언제든 변수에 노출될 수 있다. 시장 참가자들은 봉쇄 여부보다 실제 통항량과 보험·운임의 움직임을 더 면밀히 지켜보고 있다.

US Navy says Hormuz is not closed, keeps monitoring to keep the lane open

The US military denied claims that the Strait of Hormuz had been closed, saying it continues to monitor the strait to protect freedom of navigation. As a chokepoint for global crude flows, markets are watching closely.
By Ma Yak-ran · 2026.06.21

The US Navy rejected some reports that the Strait of Hormuz had been shut, saying transit is normal and US forces are continuously monitoring the situation to ensure the lane's safety and freedom of navigation.

광고 문의 · 300×250

The Strait of Hormuz is a strategic chokepoint through which a large share of the world's seaborne crude passes. Whenever Middle East tensions flare, talk of closure surfaces and energy markets react sensitively.

Closure rumors and fact-checking

The statement is read as an attempt to calm anxiety over a possible closure. Authorities stressed that merchant transit has continued and that they maintain a monitoring posture to respond to any threat.

Still, with negotiations and military tension running in parallel, the strait's stability remains exposed to variables. Market participants are watching actual transit volumes and moves in insurance and freight more closely than the question of closure itself.

Hwagyo Times notes that stability in Hormuz bears directly on energy costs in Korea and East Asia. Rather than being swayed by fragments of rumor, prudence calls for checking both the lane's status and market indicators.
