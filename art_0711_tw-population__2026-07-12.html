Taiwan's Population Falls for a 30th Straight Month

Taiwan's population declined for a 30th consecutive month, as deaths outpacing births hardens into a trend — deepening structural challenges for the workforce, military manpower and schools.
By Kang Pae-san · 2026.07.12

The population extended its slide to 30 months, the Taipei Times reported.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Monthly births kept trailing deaths, against a backdrop of fewer marriages and rising first-marriage ages.

Authorities have rolled out childcare and housing-cost measures, but no clear reversal is in sight.

The trajectory mirrors ultra-low fertility in Korea and Japan, pushing immigration and foreign-labor policy redesign up the shared agenda.

Over time the shift will also reshape the generational makeup of overseas-Chinese schools and communities.
