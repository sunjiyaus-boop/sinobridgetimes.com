A third of Russia's refining capacity knocked out; petrol shortages spread across more than half its regions

Ukrainian drone strikes have idled roughly a third of Russia's oil-refining capacity, causing petrol shortages across more than half of Russia's regions, Western media reported.
By Jin Tae-heung · 2026.07.17

Ukraine has repeatedly hit refineries and depots deep in Russia's rear with long-range drones, making energy infrastructure a battlefield of its own apart from the attritional front line.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Fuel queues and purchase limits have been reported in some regions, with authorities responding through export controls on refined products and rationing-style measures.

The fuel crunch could feed through logistics costs into inflation, and a prolonged shortage would weigh on the Russian economy, observers say.

Global crude prices and refining margins are feeling the effects, and analysts note widened export opportunities for Asian refiners.
