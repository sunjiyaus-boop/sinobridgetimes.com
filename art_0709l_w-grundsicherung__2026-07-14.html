이문창 기자 · 2026.07.14

Germany Scraps Bürgergeld for Stricter 'New Basic Security' as Merz Targets Welfare Abuse

Germany abolished its Bürgergeld long-term unemployment benefit, replacing it on July 1 with Neue Grundsicherung, while Chancellor Merz rolls out an action plan against benefit abuse.
By Lee Mun-chang · 2026.07.14

The new rules tighten eligibility and toughen sanctions: missing two Jobcentre appointments cuts support 30%; refusing reasonable work can suspend benefits for two months.

광고 문의 · 300×250

A savings grace period is abolished — applicants with significant savings must spend their own funds first.

Merz insists no one will become homeless and frames the reform as promoting a return to work.

Welfare groups object; the VdK warns housing-cost caps risk rent arrears and, at worst, homelessness.

Austerity in Europe's largest economy will be a reference for welfare debates elsewhere; migrant communities in Germany are among those affected.
