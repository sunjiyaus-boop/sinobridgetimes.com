Kaohsiung's street parasols shade the sun and shed the rain, open around the clock

Large parasols Kaohsiung installed for pedestrians are winning praise for blocking blazing sun and sudden showers alike, Liberty Times reported; the metro bureau says they stay open 24 hours and fold only in strong wind.
By Kang Pae-san · 2026.07.05

According to Liberty Times, the parasols stand at crosswalk waiting zones near metro stations, easing the wait.

광고 문의 · 300×250

The Taipei Times reported other cities are also expanding shade installations.

As extreme heat becomes routine, 'shade welfare' is emerging as a yardstick of urban governance.

Maintenance costs and typhoon-season safety are the noted challenges.

The policy mirrors crosswalk canopies in Seoul and elsewhere in Korea, showing how heat-response ideas travel between cities.
