In developing humanoid robots for productivity, a 'de-humanizing' trend is emerging that no longer insists on a human shape.
By Bang Mun-yeon · 2026.06.30

Per the Chosun Ilbo, some robotics developers, to raise efficiency, are adopting task-optimized forms—wheels, multi-jointed arms—rather than clinging to a human look, focusing on 'doing the job well' over 'resembling a person.'

Industrial robots excel at repeating set tasks with precision; designs that prioritize function over form raise efficiency on the production floor.

A robot's form evolves with its purpose; the recognition that humanoid shapes aren't optimal for every task is changing design philosophy.
