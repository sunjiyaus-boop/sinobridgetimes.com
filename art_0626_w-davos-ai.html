Summer Davos puts AI at the center, weighing ‘technology and growth’

The World Economic Forum's summer meeting put artificial intelligence at the heart of its agenda, discussing paths for technology and growth.
By Jo Gye-hyeon · 2026.06.26

The WEF's summer meeting convened with artificial intelligence as a core theme, China's Huanqiu reported, as government and business figures discussed AI's impact on growth, jobs and industrial structure.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Participants agreed AI carries potential to lift productivity alongside challenges of job transition and widening gaps, with the focus on matching the pace of innovation to social safety nets.

International cooperation and competition over AI supply chains, semiconductors and data and power infrastructure also featured heavily, with the key question being how to widen space for mutually beneficial cooperation amid intensifying tech rivalry.

East Asia is a core hub for AI chips and manufacturing, so the direction of these talks carries direct implications for Korea, Taiwan and China.
