EU to Delay Methane-Violation Penalties by Three Years, Drawing 'Another Climate Retreat' Criticism

The EU will postpone enforcement of penalties for methane-emission violations by three years, prompting criticism that climate policy has stepped back again. We present climate and policy matters with balance.
By Jeon Hong-bal · 2026.07.23

The EU decided to grant a three-year grace period before applying penalties for methane-rule violations, Chosun Ilbo reported, read as accounting for industry burdens and economic conditions.

광고 문의 · 300×250

Methane is a potent greenhouse gas with a stronger short-term warming effect than CO2; cutting it is seen as an important pillar of climate goals.

A penalty delay has the rationale of giving regulated parties time to adapt, but critics say it saps momentum for cuts.

Nations are calibrating policy pace among climate goals, industrial competitiveness and prices; predictability and consistency of rules are key.

We view climate policy within the tension of ideals and reality, laying facts side by side and leaving to readers whether this is retreat or adjustment.
